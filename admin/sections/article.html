Analytics For Websites Built With Webflow

Knowing what’s happening on your website is an extremely important part of growing your business. How can you tell what solution on your website is working and what’s not working if you have no data? These are some steps we usually take on our websites.

1. Set up Google Analytics

To make sure you’re tracking the right actions, please consult with your marketing team. But the general rules are quite simple—you want to track any actions which indicate that the user is interested in you. We usually track interactions with CTA buttons, depth of scroll and the user’s journey through the site.

3. Set up Facebook and Google Pixel

Please install Facebook and Google Pixels the very moment the development is finished. You might not be interested in launching an advertising campaign right now but you might be in a few months or years. This way, you’ll be tracking users that visited your website from day one and Pixels will be able to tell which of them were interested in your company. This will help you make your advertising more precise and, therefore, more profitable.

4. Set up Clarity or HotJar

What if there was a tool which allowed you to watch users on your website in real time? Or what if there was a way to record user sessions and watch their behaviour and actions on your website? Sounds almost illegal and impossible, right? Yet it does exist and it’s not illegal! Please meet Clarity from Microsoft and HotJar! Those tools give you the most precise picture of your users’ behaviour!

With these tools, you can actually see how your users behave on a website, with which elements they interact and where they spend most of their time! It is truly precious information which might help you improve your conversion rate and user experience.
